## The exp30 arc (all n=45, real de-dipped MAH, ALL radii, median profile max|rel|)
1. **Gate — core-retaining transport** (`transport_floor.py`): deposit = retained core
   + migrated envelope, mass-conserving, Δt-only observation dependence, CoG linear in
   masses → NNLS inner solve. Additive floor 18.5% → transport 9.1%. GATE PASSED.
2. **2.1 factorial** ({clock} × {width form}): the intuitive "combine the winners"
   model collapsed onto envelope (11.7%) — completing the 2×2 exposed the untested
   cell as the true winner: **dyntrans** (dynamical clock τ=α·tᵢ + multi-scale
   per-deposit width) **7.5%**, 4 params, best at every epoch, best by every IC
   (`ic_compare.py`: k_eff=14 incl. ~10 active NNLS masses). Fitted α≈1.
3. **2.2 + 2.2b events** (`event_kicks.py`): kicks at real-MAH bursts UNDERPERFORM
   (10.3% vs 7.5%; pre-test null: α ⊥ burstiness). User's coalescence-delay hypothesis
   tested: lag t'=(1+β)t_j genuinely helps (→9.5%) with a coherent physical
   **β=0.37 (IQR 0.30–0.72 ≈ dynamical friction)** — yet still loses. **τ≈tᵢ is the
   delay-averaged merger clock**; discreteness adds nothing in-sample.
4. **2.3 LOEO** (`holdout.py`): the in-sample ranking INVERTS out-of-sample —
   dyntrans-free 7.5%→53.7% held-out (gap +46); additive 30.9 (+11); loose 35.3 (+26).
   **Free NNLS masses are the generalization liability** (they absorb epoch-specific
   information); totals predict fine, the shape overfits.
5. **Phase 3 v1 — the fix** (`param_emulator.py`): dyntrans structure + two-epoch
   efficiency masses dM*ᵢ = f(zᵢ)·dMhᵢ → **7 params, ZERO free masses**. In-sample
   **9.7%** (only +2.2 over free masses); **LOEO held-avg 24.0% (gap +14.3), incl.
   the z=0.4 forward holdout (31.4%)** — beats every 2.3 model at every epoch.
   Fitted params physical: **α=1.01 (self-similar clock, 3rd independent
   confirmation)**, q=0.77, b_early=4.48, b_late=1.88, z_c=2.23. **v2 gate CLOSED**:
   residuals ⊥ MAH burstiness (ρ≈0) — no evidence for a dual-region ex-situ channel
   at current precision (the physics idea, from the user, stays parked with the β
   measurement as its calibration if reopened).
