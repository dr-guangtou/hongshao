## Next session's task: the exp25 population fit

exp25 built a physics-inspired **deposition-kernel toy** that reconstructs a
galaxy's 1-D stellar profile from its *actual* halo MAH, and validated it on the
single most massive TNG300 galaxy (CoG RMS 0.008 dex). **The next step is to fit
it across the population** and ask whether the parameters are universal вҖ” and in
particular whether the quenching redshift `z_c` scales with halo mass.

## The model (frozen for the population fit)
For one galaxy, with main-branch MAH `M_h(t_i)` at snapshots (redshift `z_i`,
cosmic time `t_i`):
- halo-mass increments `dM_h,i = max(M_h(t_i) вҲ’ M_h(t_{i-1}), 0)`;
- per-epoch stellar mass `dM*_i = Оө(z_i)В·dM_h,i`, normalized so `ОЈ dM*_i = M*_tot`;
- each epoch deposits a centred 2-D Gaussian; closed-form CoG:
  `M*(<R) = ОЈ_i dM*_i (1 вҲ’ exp(вҲ’RВІ/2Пғ_iВІ))`, density `ОЈ(R) = ОЈ_i dM*_i/(2ПҖПғ_iВІ) exp(вҲ’RВІ/2Пғ_iВІ)`.

Two smooth ingredients (5 parameters):
- **width** `Пғ(t) = Пғ_0 (t/t_obs)^g`  вҖ” *(R_200c was tested and dropped: a direct
  Пғ(t) fits equally well without the M_peak=M_200c assumption.)*
- **efficiency** (two-epoch quenching, continuous at `z_c`):
  `Оө вҲқ (1+z)^b_early` for `z вүҘ z_c`; `Оө вҲқ (1+z_c)^(b_earlyвҲ’b_late)(1+z)^b_late` for `z < z_c`.

Single-galaxy best fit (most massive): `Пғ_0вүҲ71 kpc, gвүҲ1.3, b_earlyвүҲ8.7, z_cвүҲ5.0,
b_lateвүҲ1.1` вҶ’ 0.008 dex.

## Population-fit plan
1. **Select the sample.** Use the clean `use` cut (nвүҲ2539); the fit needs each
   galaxy's MAH (`load_mah`/`peak_history`/`load_cosmic_time`) and its CoG
   (`logmstar_cog`). The most massive galaxy code is in
   `experiments/exp25_deposition_kernel/run.py` вҖ” generalize its `fit_two_epoch`
   to loop over galaxies (validate small first, e.g. 50 galaxies, sub-minute).
2. **Fit the 5 params per galaxy.** Watch the known fragilities: `z_c`/`b_early`
   degeneracy on a single object, and Оө>1 at the earliest epochs (cap at the
   baryon fraction `f_b=0.157`, or bound `b_early`). Multistart on `z_c` (the
   single-galaxy code already does).
3. **The headline questions:**
   - Are `Пғ_0, g, b_early, b_late` ~universal (tight distributions), or do they
     vary systematically?
   - **Does `z_c` scale with halo mass?** The quenching picture predicts massive
     halos quench earlier вҶ’ `z_c` rises with `logM_h`. Plot `z_c` vs `logmh_z0p4`
     / `dmah_logmp`; fit `z_c = z_c0 + sВ·(logM_h вҲ’ pivot)`.
   - Does fixing the "universal" params and letting only `(z_c, normalization)`
     vary still reproduce the profiles? (the real test of a population model.)
4. **Compare** the toy's accuracy/scatter to the data-driven emulator's
   per-radius CRPS (exp22) as a sanity baseline вҖ” but remember the toy's value is
   *interpretability + a forward MAHвҶ’profile map*, not beating the emulator.

## Watch out / gotchas
- **Оө вүӨ f_b.** The unconstrained fit pushes Оө>1 at zвүі8 (2.8% of M* for the most
